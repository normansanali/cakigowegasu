Jon Favreau Biography

Jonathan Kolia Favreau was born in Flushing, New York, on October 19, 1956, to Charles and Madeleine Favreau. He went to Hebrew school.

He graduated from The Bronx High School of Science in 1984. He attended Queens College but left before graduating. Following that, he relocated to Chicago to pursue a career in comedy. He also temporarily worked at Bear Stearns on Wall Street.

Jon Favreau Height, Weight

Jon Favreau’s height is 6′ 1″ in feet and inches or 185.5 centimeters, and his weight is 196 pounds or 89 kilograms.

Career

Favreau made his cinematic debut in 1993 with the biographical sports film ‘Rudy.’ The film’s topic was Daniel Ruettiger, an American motivational speaker and footballer.

Over the following several years, he acted in various films, including ‘Mrs. Parker and the Vicious Circle,’ ‘Very Bad Things,’ and ‘Love & Sex.’ With the film ‘Made,’ he made his directing debut in 2001.

He directed the sequel to the immensely popular ‘Iron Man’ in 2010. It was also nominated for a ‘Oscar’ in the category of ‘Best Visual Effects.’

He went on to feature in movies including ‘Entourage,’ ‘Term Life,’ and ‘Spider-Man: Homecoming.’ In addition, he directed the Oscar-winning adventure picture ‘The Jungle Book,’ which was released in 2016.

In 2018, he co-executive produced ‘Avengers: Infinity War’. The 2019 picture ‘Avengers: Endgame,’ directed by the Russo brothers, was executive produced by Favreau. He also appeared in ‘Spider-Man: Far From Home.’
