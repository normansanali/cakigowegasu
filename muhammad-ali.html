Muhammad Ali Career

Muhammad Ali is a legend and the greatest name in the sport of boxing. The most productive figure in boxing history is well-known for his outstanding feet inside the ring, his quick footwork, powerful jab, and a variety of other abilities. He began his career by competing in the Golden Gloves tournament in 1956. Additionally, he won the light-heavyweight division in the National Golden Gloves Tournament of Champions. After putting up an incredible game performance, he was invited to play in the 1960 Olympics, where he won gold.

Continuing his quest to become a world-class boxer, he overcame Sonny Liston to claim the reigning heavyweight title. He successfully defended the title by defeating notable opponents such as Sonny Liston, Floyd Patterson, George Chuvalo, Henry Cooper, Brian London, Karl Mildenberger, Cleveland Williams, and Terrell. He was stripped of the title, however, after refusing to serve in the US Army during the Vietnam War. However, after serving the suspension, he returned to fight for the title against Joe Frazier in what was dubbed the Fight of the Century due to the fact that it included two undefeated opponents. However, he was defeated.

He won 56 matches and lost five during his professional career. He was dubbed The Greatest, Fighter of the Year, Sportsman of the Year, Sportsman of the Century, and Sports Personality of the Century for his spectacular record-breaking accomplishments. Additionally, he has been inducted into the International Boxing Hall of Fame, the Hollywood Walk of Fame, and numerous other halls of fame.
