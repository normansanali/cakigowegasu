Richarlison Chest Tattoo Meaning And Design; Meet His Parents Antnio Carlos Andrade &amp; Vera Lucia

Richarlison tattoos on his chest and other body parts have specific meanings. Learn about his inks, parents, childhood, and more.

Richarlison de Andrade, or Richarlison, plays as a forward for the Brazil national team (#7) and Premier League club Tottenham Hotspur (#9).

Since 2018, he has earned 39 caps and scored 19 goals as part of the senior team.

The Brazilian forward has had an explosive start to the 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar, with a brace in the opening game against Serbia.

At the club level, Richarlison played for Brazilian clubs América Mineiro and Fluminense before moving to the English league, where he played for Watford and Everton.

Richarlison Tattoo Meaning And Design

Richarlison has a fitting heart-shaped tattoo on his left chest. At the heart’s center is the Brazilian globe, where we can find the country’s flag.

The tattoo clarifies the love and dedication the footballer has toward his nation.

Richarlison is no short of tattoos on his body. He has several inks on other different parts.

We can find a vertical tattoo on his neck that reads, “He’s Brazilian.” As shared on his Instagram, he got that piece from a famous tattoo artist called Dom Tattoo.

Likewise, the same artist did his left thigh tattoo, which is the “eternalizing” of the Brazilian national team’s achievement after winning a gold medal at the 2020 Olympic tournament.

Brazil successfully retained their title, and Richarlison was the top scorer with five goals. It was a memorable tournament for the young footballer.

Before the competition, he had promised to do this and kept his word.

Likewise, Richarlison has a bunch of cartoons tattooed on his left forearm. It includes Looney Tunes character Taz, Dragon Ball Z’s Goku, Donald Duck, and Wile E. Coyote from the Roadrunner.

On his biceps of the same hand, he has more characters like Daffy Duck, Roadrunner, Popeye, etc.

Meet His Parents Antônio Carlos Andrade & Vera Lucia

Richarlison de Andrade was born in Nova Venécia in the south-eastern state of Espirito Santo, Brazil, to his parents Antônio Carlos Andrade and Vera Lucia.

He came from a lower-income family, with his Father working as a stonemason and his Mother a cleaner and ice cream seller.

Richarlison was the oldest among five children in his family. He has four brothers and one sister.

One of his brothers, Rian, shared that Richarlison inspired him as he made his first steps into European football.

As we can guess, Richarlison had a lot of responsibility growing up. He helped his parents make money by selling ice cream, chocolates and washing cars while his friends sold drugs.

The friends also tried to tempt Richarlison into their Company. They invited him to make easy money by selling drugs, but he never succumbed to criminal life.

Moreover, his mom and dad gave him a good moral upbringing, and Richarlison says he is grateful for their belief and support.

When Richarlison was seven, his dad bought him ten balls because he wanted his son to be a good footballer. He used to play with his friends in the street and have a good time.

From an early age, he dreamed of playing in the Premier League. He said, “I used to watch the Premier League on my uncle’s TV. I was in love with what I saw — the speed of the game and the way it never stopped.”
