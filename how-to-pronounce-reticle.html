What is reticle etymology?

reticle (n.)

1650s, from Latin reticulum “little net,” diminutive of rete “net” (see reticulate (adj.)).

What is the meaning of reticule?

noun. (in the 18th and 19th centuries) a woman’s small bag or purse, usually in the form of a pouch with a drawstring and made of net, beading, brocade, etc. a variant of reticle.
