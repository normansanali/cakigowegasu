The Top 10 Mets Players of All Time

When it comes to the New York Mets, there have been many great players who have donned the blue and orange over the years. From their inception in 1962 to the present day, the Mets have had some true legends grace their roster. In this article, we will take a look at the top 10 Mets players of all time and celebrate their contributions to the team's rich history.

1. Tom Seaver

Known as "The Franchise," Tom Seaver is undoubtedly the greatest player in Mets history. He spent 12 seasons with the team, winning three Cy Young Awards and leading the Mets to their first World Series title in 1969. Seaver's dominance on the mound, along with his leadership and competitive spirit, make him the top Mets player of all time.

2. Mike Piazza

Considered one of the greatest offensive catchers in baseball history, Mike Piazza had a tremendous impact during his time with the Mets. He played for the team from 1998 to 2005 and was a key player in their run to the 2000 World Series. Piazza's power hitting and ability to come through in clutch moments endeared him to Mets fans and solidified his place as one of the franchise's all-time greats.

3. David Wright

David Wright is the epitome of a Mets lifer. Spending his entire 14-year career with the team, Wright became the face of the franchise. Known for his leadership, defensive prowess, and clutch hitting, he was a seven-time All-Star and played a pivotal role in the Mets' run to the World Series in 2015. Wright's loyalty and dedication to the organization make him a beloved figure among Mets fans.

4. Dwight Gooden

Dwight Gooden burst onto the scene in the 1980s and quickly became one of the most dominant pitchers in baseball. His electric stuff and overpowering fastball earned him the nickname "Dr. K." Gooden won the National League Rookie of the Year award in 1984 and followed it up with a Cy Young Award in 1985. His tenure with the Mets was marked by brilliance on the mound and a World Series victory in 1986.

5. Darryl Strawberry

Darryl Strawberry was a force to be reckoned with during his time with the Mets. Known for his immense power and sweet swing, Strawberry was a key component of the Mets' championship teams in the 1980s. He hit 252 home runs in a Mets uniform and was selected to the All-Star team seven times. Strawberry's combination of power and charisma made him a fan favorite and solidified his place in Mets history.

6. Jerry Koosman

Jerry Koosman was a vital member of the Mets' pitching staff during their championship seasons in 1969 and 1986. He formed a formidable duo with Tom Seaver and played a crucial role in the team's success. Koosman was a two-time All-Star and finished second in the Cy Young Award voting in 1968. His contributions to the Mets' pitching staff make him one of the greatest players in franchise history.

7. Keith Hernandez

Keith Hernandez brought his exceptional defensive skills and leadership to the Mets when he was acquired in a trade in 1983. Known for his smooth glove work at first base, Hernandez won six consecutive Gold Glove Awards from 1983 to 1988. He was also a key contributor on offense, hitting for both average and power. Hernandez's impact on the Mets' success cannot be understated, and he remains a beloved figure among fans.

8. Carlos Beltrán

Carlos Beltrán had a memorable tenure with the Mets from 2005 to 2011. Known for his all-around skills, Beltrán was an exceptional defensive center fielder and a consistent offensive threat. He was selected to the All-Star team five times as a Met and won three Gold Glove Awards. Beltrán's contributions on both sides of the ball made him one of the best players in Mets history.

9. John Franco

John Franco holds the record for the most saves in Mets history with 276. He pitched for the team from 1990 to 2004 and was a reliable closer throughout his career. Franco's leadership and consistency on the mound earned him the respect of his teammates and fans alike. His impact on the Mets' bullpen and his status as the team's all-time saves leader make him one of the top players in franchise history.

10. José Reyes

José Reyes brought excitement and energy to the Mets during his time with the team. Known for his blazing speed and electrifying plays, Reyes was a fan favorite. He won the National League batting title in 2011 and was named to four All-Star teams as a Met. Reyes' dynamic playstyle and infectious enthusiasm endeared him to Mets fans and secured his spot on this list.

The New York Mets have been fortunate to have many talented and impactful players throughout their history. From Tom Seaver to José Reyes, these players have left an indelible mark on the franchise and have provided countless memorable moments for fans. While this list represents the top 10 Mets players of all time, it is important to recognize and appreciate the contributions of all the players who have worn the blue and orange.
