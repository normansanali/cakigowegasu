Jeff Hardy Makes His AEW Return; First Appearance Since DUI Case

Jeff Hardy is one of the most influential wrestlers on today's upcoming generation of stars. He made a name for himself in WWE for his death-defying stunts and eye-catching style. He and his brother Matt revolutionized tag team wrestling and brought it to the forefront of the business when it was once an afterthought. RELATED: AEW's Dax Harwood Says CM Punk & FTR Vs. The Elite Could Sell 50,000 Tickets For All InJeff made his AEW debut in March 2022 and reunited with his brother Matt to reform the Hardy Boyz. However, the reunion tour ended abruptly on June 12, 2022, when Jeff Hardy was arrested and charged with DUI. It was his third offense within the past ten years. As a result, Hardy served 38 days in jail, along with having his license suspended and having to pay about $4,500 in fines.

Jeff Hardy Returns To AEW After 10-Month Suspension

On tonight's episode of AEW Dynamite, Jeff Hardy made his return to the company and to public life. He came to the aid of his brother Matt, Hook, and Isiah Kassidy as the trio was being attacked by Ethan Page and the Firm. Jeff received a massive, excited ovation from the AEW fans in attendance. He came to the ring with a steel chair that he painted himself and used it to subdue his opponents before hitting Lee Moriarty with his signature Swanton Bomb.

While fans are, of course, excited to see Jeff Hardy on television again, there is an undercurrent of worry about the legend's health. Sobriety, after all, is not a linear road. We wish him nothing but continued health, happiness, and safety as he seeks to pick up where he left off alongside Matt in AEW.
